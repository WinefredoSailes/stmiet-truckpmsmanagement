Chapter 4: Truck Registration & Documentation

4.1 Overview

Every truck in the fleet must be registered in the system with complete Certificate of Registration (CR) documentation. Proper registration ensures that all PM schedules are auto-generated, service history is tracked per vehicle, and regulatory compliance documents are accessible at all times.

4.4 Registering a New Truck

Responsible Role: Admin or Super Admin

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → Trucks.
  2. Click the "Add Truck" button.
  3. Complete all required fields (marked with an asterisk). Fill in optional fields where data is available.
  4. Scan and attach CR documents if applicable.
  5. Set the initial Status to "Active" if the truck is operational.
  6. Enter the current Mileage (km) and Engine Hours from the truck's odometer and hour meter.
  7. Click "Save".
  8. Important: The system automatically generates 154 PM schedules for the new truck based on the standard task templates. This process runs in the background after saving.
  9. Verify the PM schedules were created by navigating to Sidebar → PMS Schedules and filtering by the new truck.
Note on Mileage and Engine Hours: Accurate initial values are critical. Every PM schedule's "next due" calculation starts from these baseline readings. If the truck has existing service records, enter the last-known PM completion data into the respective PM schedule records after creation.

4.5 Editing a Truck Record

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → Trucks.
  2. Click the truck you wish to edit (opens the Detail page).
  3. Click the "Edit" button.
  4. Update the desired fields. Note: Changing mileage or engine hours here will affect all PM schedule due-date calculations.
  5. Click "Save".

Chapter 5: Fleet Status Management

5.1 Truck Status Definitions

StatusDefinitionImplications
ActiveTruck is fully operational and available for dispatchPM schedules are active. Job Orders can be created.
DownTruck is currently out of service due to breakdown or major repairCannot be dispatched. PM schedules remain active (mechanical issues may be PM-related).
RetiredTruck has been permanently decommissioned, sold, or scrappedNo new Job Orders can be created. PM schedules are frozen but retained for historical reference.

5.2 Changing Truck Status

  1. Navigate to the truck's Detail page.
  2. Click "Edit".
  3. Change the Status field.
  4. Optionally add a note in the description field explaining the reason for the status change.
  5. Click "Save".
Best Practice: When marking a truck as "Down," create a corresponding Job Order to track the repair work causing the downtime. This ensures the downtime is documented and can be analyzed in KPI reports.

5.3 Viewing Fleet Summary

The Dashboard shows a quick summary of fleet status:

  • Active Trucks: Total count of trucks with Active status.
  • Overdue / Due PM: List of trucks with PM items that are due or overdue.
  • Open Job Orders: Count of all JOs currently Open or In Progress.

For a full fleet overview, navigate to Sidebar → Trucks to see all trucks in a sortable, searchable table.

5.4 Syncing New PM Templates to Existing Trucks

If new PM task templates are added to the system after trucks have already been registered, the new templates must be synced to existing trucks:

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → PMS Schedules.
  2. Click the "Sync All Trucks" button.
  3. The system will create any missing PM schedule records for all trucks.
  4. To sync a single truck: open the truck's Detail page and click the Refresh icon in the PM Schedule section.
