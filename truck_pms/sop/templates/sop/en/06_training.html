Volume 6 — Training & On-the-Job Training (OJT)

Chapter 18: Trainee Onboarding & System Access

18.1 Overview of the Training Program

The OJT/Training module tracks the progress of apprentice mechanics and trainees as they learn the trade. The system supports three aspects of training:

  • Attendance tracking: Daily check-in and check-out to monitor presence.
  • Task ratings: Supervisors rate OJTs on specific PM/repair tasks they perform (1-5 scale).
  • Weekly reviews: Supervisors provide structured evaluations with strengths, areas for improvement, and overall scores.

18.2 Setting Up a New Trainee

Responsible Role: Admin or Super Admin

Step 1 — Create User Account:

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → User Management.
  2. Click "Add User".
  3. Set Role = "OJT / Trainee."
  4. Complete all required fields (username, password, names, etc.).
  5. Click "Save".

Step 2 — Assign to Training:

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → Assign Training.
  2. Select the OJT you just created from the dropdown.
  3. Select their Supervisor — this must be a user with Staff, Admin, or Super Admin role. The supervisor will be responsible for rating and reviewing the OJT.
  4. Set the Start Date — typically the OJT's first day at the workshop.
  5. Click "Assign".

The OJT can now log in and access the Training Dashboard. The supervisor can see the OJT in their supervisor dashboard.

18.3 Supervisor Selection Guidelines

The supervisor plays a critical role in the OJT's development. Choose a supervisor who:

  • Has at least 2 years of experience in truck maintenance.
  • Is patient and communicates well.
  • Can dedicate time each week to fill out ratings and reviews.
  • Is proficient in using the Truck PMS system.

A supervisor may oversee multiple OJTs, but should not have more than 3 OJTs assigned at one time to maintain quality of mentorship.

Chapter 19: Daily Attendance Procedure

19.1 How Attendance Works

Attendance is recorded daily. Each OJT checks in when they arrive and checks out when they leave. The system records timestamps and prevents duplicate check-ins on the same day.

19.2 OJT — Checking In

  1. Log into the system (redirected to Training Dashboard automatically).
  2. On the Attendance card, click "Check In".
  3. The current time is recorded as time_in.
  4. A confirmation message appears: "Checked in at [time]."

19.3 OJT — Checking Out

  1. Before leaving for the day, ensure all tasks or training activities for the day are complete.
  2. On the Attendance card, click "Check Out".
  3. The current time is recorded as time_out.
  4. A confirmation message appears: "Checked out at [time]."

19.4 Attendance Rules

  • An OJT can only check in once per day. If they forget to check in, they should notify their supervisor to record the attendance manually.
  • Checking out is optional but recommended. An OJT who forgets to check out can still check out the next day (the system records the previous day's check-out time).
  • If an OJT is absent, no attendance record is created for that day. The supervisor may note the absence in the weekly review.

19.5 Attendance Reports (Supervisor Access)

Supervisors can view an OJT's attendance history from the Training Dashboard or by navigating to the specific OJT's profile page. The attendance log shows each day's check-in and check-out times, with total hours calculated.

Chapter 20: Task Rating & Skills Assessment

20.1 Purpose of Task Ratings

Task ratings track an OJT's progress on specific maintenance tasks. Each rating records:

  • What task was performed (linked to PM task template or custom task name).
  • Who rated them (the supervisor).
  • A numerical score (1-5).
  • Comments or observations.

20.2 Rating Scale

ScoreLabelDescription
1NoviceRequires constant supervision. Cannot perform task independently. Needs significant guidance on safety, tool selection, and procedure.
2BeginnerCan perform task with step-by-step guidance. Knows the general process but needs coaching on technique and efficiency.
3CompetentCan perform task independently with minimal supervision. Follows the correct procedure. Completes work within reasonable time. Understands safety requirements.
4ProficientPerforms task efficiently and consistently. Can identify and correct common issues without assistance. Understands underlying principles. Good diagnostic ability.
5ExpertCan perform task flawlessly. Can teach others. Able to handle complex or unusual variations. Understands the system-level impact of the task.

20.3 Creating a Task Rating (Supervisor)

  1. Navigate to the Training Dashboard or the specific OJT's profile.
  2. Click "New Rating".
  3. Complete the form:
    • OJT: Select the trainee you are rating.
    • Rating: Select 1-5 based on the scale above.
    • PM Task: Optional. If the OJT performed a specific PM task, select it from the dropdown (populated from the 154 PM templates). This links the rating to a standardized task for progress tracking.
    • Task Name (Custom): If the task is not a standard PM template, type a custom description. Example: "Assisted with transmission removal," "Practiced diagnostic scan tool usage."
    • Comments: Provide specific, actionable feedback. Mention what was done well AND what needs improvement. Avoid vague comments like "Good job" — instead say "Performed brake lining inspection correctly, measured all positions, identified the right-rear as needing replacement. Needs to work on speed — took 45 minutes vs. the standard 20 minutes."
  4. Click "Save Rating".
  5. The OJT can view the rating on their Training Dashboard.

20.4 Rating Guidelines for Supervisors

  • Rate frequently: Aim for at least 3-5 ratings per OJT per week. Frequent, small feedback is more effective than infrequent, lengthy evaluations.
  • Rate promptly: Complete the rating while the task is still fresh in your mind — ideally within 2 hours of the OJT completing the task.
  • Be objective: Base the rating on the OJT's demonstrated performance on that specific task, not on your general impression of them. A competent mechanic may still be a novice at a specific new task.
  • Include positive and constructive feedback: Even a score of 3 (Competent) should note what was done correctly plus one area for improvement.
  • Use the system: Ratings feed into the OJT's average score and provide a quantitative record of skill development over time.

Chapter 21: Weekly Review & Progress Tracking

21.1 Purpose of Weekly Reviews

Weekly reviews provide a structured, holistic evaluation of the OJT's progress. Unlike task ratings (which are task-specific), the weekly review covers overall performance, attitude, safety awareness, and professional development.

21.2 Creating a Weekly Review (Supervisor)

  1. Navigate to the Training Dashboard or the specific OJT's profile.
  2. Click "New Review".
  3. Complete the form:
    • OJT: Select the trainee.
    • Week Start / Week End: The Monday-to-Sunday (or applicable work week) period being reviewed.
    • Overall Score: 1-5 scale. This is a holistic score for the week, considering: task performance, safety behavior, punctuality, teamwork, attitude, and initiative. See the rating scale in Chapter 20.2 for guidance.
    • Strengths: What did the OJT do well this week? Be specific: "Demonstrated strong initiative by organizing the tool cart without being asked. Showed good understanding of brake system inspection procedure."
    • Areas for Improvement: What specific skills or behaviors need development? "Needs to improve speed on oil changes — target is under 30 minutes including documentation. Also needs to remember to replace the drain plug gasket every time — missed it twice this week."
    • Supervisor Notes: Any additional comments, concerns, or recommendations. This may include: recommendations for additional training, observations about attitude or teamwork, safety incidents or near-misses, notable achievements.
  4. Click "Save Review".

21.3 Review Cadence

PeriodReview FrequencyFocus
Weeks 1-4 (First Month)WeeklySafety awareness, tool familiarity, basic skills, workshop orientation, punctuality, attitude
Weeks 5-12 (Months 2-3)Every 2 weeksSkill development on specific PM procedures, diagnostic thinking, speed vs. quality balance

21.6 Interpreting OJT Progress

Use the trend in average scores over time to assess development:

  • Upward trend (scores increasing over weeks): OJT is learning and developing as expected.
  • Flat trend at 3-4 (scores consistently in the Competent-Proficient range): OJT is making solid progress. Look for opportunities to introduce more challenging tasks.
  • Flat trend at 1-2 (scores staying at Novice-Beginner after 4+ weeks): This is a red flag. Investigate: Is the OJT receiving adequate instruction? Are the tasks too advanced for their current level? Is there an attitude or motivation issue? Consider adjusting the training approach or having a direct conversation with the OJT.
  • Downward trend (scores decreasing): Investigate immediately. Possible causes: boredom or lack of challenge, personal issues affecting performance, inadequate supervision, or a mismatch between the OJT and the trade.
Note on OJT Role & System Access: OJT/Trainee accounts have the most restricted access in the system. They can only see the Training Dashboard, truck list (read-only), and their own ratings and reviews. They cannot create or modify any operational records. This is intentional — their role is to learn, not to perform unsupervised work.
