Volume 5 — Contractor Management

Chapter 15: Contractor Onboarding & Skills Registry

15.1 When to Use a Contractor

Contractors are external service providers used when:

  • The required repair or service is outside the in-house mechanics' expertise (e.g., calibration, specialized welding, electrical diagnostics for OEM systems).
  • In-house capacity is fully utilized and the job cannot be deferred.
  • The job requires specialized equipment not available in the workshop (e.g., hydrostatic testing, ultrasonic thickness gauging, dynamometer testing).
  • Warranty or insurance requirements mandate OEM-certified technicians.

15.2 Registering a New Contractor in the System

Responsible Role: Admin or Super Admin

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → Contractors.
  2. Click "Add Contractor".
  3. Complete the form:
    • Company Name: Legal business name as registered with government or as stated on the invoice.
    • Contact Person: Name of the person you communicate with for scheduling and invoicing.
    • Mobile Number: Direct line to the contact person.
    • Email Address: For sending Job Orders, correspondence, and invoices.
    • Address: Physical office or workshop address.
    • Skills: Comma-separated list of services the contractor provides. Be specific: "Welding, Calibration, Electrical Diagnostics, Vapor Recovery Testing, Tank Hydrostatic Testing, Hydraulic Repairs."
    • Active: Check this box to allow the contractor to be assigned Job Orders. Uncheck to temporarily or permanently disable contractor assignments without deleting the record.
  4. Click "Save".

15.3 Contractor User Account

Each contractor company can have a user account in the system with the CONTRACTOR role. This account:

  • Can only see Job Orders assigned to their company.
  • Has the same workflow capabilities as a mechanic (mark line items Done, record parts, add hours).
  • Does not have access to any other part of the system (truck list read-only, no PM schedules, no reports).
  • Is redirected to My Assignments on login for immediate access to work orders.

To create a contractor user account, see Chapter 2 (User Account Lifecycle) — select Role = CONTRACTOR.

Chapter 16: Contractor Service Execution

16.1 Creating a Contractor Job Order

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → Job Orders → New Job Order.
  2. Select the Truck.
  3. Set Type = "Contractor Service."
  4. In the Assigned To field, select the Contractor Company (not an individual mechanic).
  5. Complete the remaining fields (title, description, priority).
  6. Click "Save".
  7. Add line items for each specific task the contractor must perform.

16.2 Contractor On-Site Procedure

  1. Check-in: When the contractor arrives at the workshop, they must:
    • Report to the supervisor or admin on duty.
    • Present any required credentials or certifications for the job.
    • Review the Job Order scope with the supervisor before starting work.
    • Be briefed on workshop safety rules, emergency exits, and the location of fire extinguishers and first aid kits.
  2. During work:
    • The contractor updates the Job Order line items in the system (or the supervisor updates if the contractor does not have system access).
    • The supervisor may perform spot checks on the work in progress.
    • Any additional work found during the job must be approved by the supervisor before proceeding (verbal approval is acceptable; documentation follows).
  3. Check-out:
    • Work is inspected and approved by the supervisor (see QC inspection in Chapter 13).
    • The contractor's service report (paper or digital) is submitted to the supervisor.
    • The supervisor signs off on the Job Order.

16.3 Invoicing & Cost Tracking

  • All parts and materials used by the contractor are recorded in the Job Order line items (see Section 12.3).
  • The contractor's labor cost is recorded as the contractor's quoted rate in the line item notes or as a separate part entry for "labor charge."
  • Upon completion, the contractor submits an invoice referencing the Job Order number.
  • The supervisor verifies the invoice against the Job Order (line items, parts, hours) and approves for payment.

17.2 Evaluating Contractor Quality

In addition to quantitative metrics, evaluate contractors on:

  • Work quality: Does the work pass QC inspection on the first attempt? Are there recurring issues that trace back to contractor work?
  • Reliability: Does the contractor show up on the scheduled date and time? Do they complete work within the estimated time?
  • Documentation: Does the contractor provide complete service reports, photos (if applicable), and accurate invoices?
  • Safety compliance: Does the contractor follow workshop safety rules? Do they wear proper PPE? Do they bring their own safety equipment?
  • Communication: Does the contractor communicate delays, parts issues, or additional findings promptly?

Evaluation notes can be recorded in the contractor's record using the Notes field available in the Contractor detail page (accessible from Sidebar → Contractors).
