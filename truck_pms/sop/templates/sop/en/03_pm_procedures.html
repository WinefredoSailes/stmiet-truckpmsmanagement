Chapter 6: PM Scheduling — Interval Types

6.1 Purpose of Preventive Maintenance

The Preventive Maintenance program ensures that every tanker truck in the fleet receives timely, scheduled service to prevent unexpected breakdowns, extend equipment life, maintain safety compliance, and comply with depot access requirements. A well-executed PM program reduces costly emergency repairs and keeps the fleet operational.

6.2 Interval Types

Each PM task in the system is assigned one of four interval types that determines when it becomes due:

TypeIconBasisExample
MILEAGE🛣️Odometer reading"Oil change every 5,000 km" — due when current_mileage ≥ last_mileage + 5,000
HOURS⏱️Engine hours meter"Belt inspection every 500 hrs" — due when current_hours ≥ last_hours + 500
CALENDAR📅Elapsed time in days"Tank hydrostatic test every 365 days" — due when today ≥ last_done + 365 days
VISUAL👁️Manual judgment"Pre-trip inspection" — always shows as "visual" status; no automatic calculation

6.3 Due Date Calculation

The system calculates PM status in real-time (no stored "due date" field). The formula for each interval type:

  • MILEAGE: current_mileage >= last_mileage_km + interval_value → DUE. A 10% threshold applies: if within 10% of interval, status shows as "Due" (yellow) instead of "Overdue."
  • HOURS: Same logic as mileage, using engine hours.
  • CALENDAR: today >= last_completed_at + timedelta(days=interval_value) → DUE. Same 10% grace threshold.
  • VISUAL: Always returns "visual" status — no auto-calculation.

6.5 How PM Schedules Update on Job Order Close

When a Job Order is closed, the system automatically:

  1. Records the truck's current mileage and engine hours at the time of close.
  2. Finds the relevant PM Schedule(s) that match the line items on the Job Order (matched by task template).
  3. Updates those PM Schedules:
    • last_completed_at = JO close timestamp
    • last_mileage_km = mileage at close
    • last_engine_hours = engine hours at close
    • status recalculated automatically
  4. Line items without a matching PM template do not update any schedule.
Important: PM schedules can also be updated manually. Click the pencil icon next to any PM schedule on the truck detail page to edit the last-completed values directly. This is useful when service was performed outside the Job Order system (e.g., third-party shop).

Chapter 7: PM Task Categories & Templates

7.1 System Structure

The PM system is organized into three levels:

  1. Task Categories (23): High-level groups of related maintenance tasks. Each category represents a system or area of the truck (e.g., "Engine PM," "Brakes," "Tires").
  2. Task Templates (154): Specific, pre-defined tasks within each category. Each template has a name, an interval value, an interval unit (km/hrs/days), and a category assignment.
  3. PM Schedules: Per-truck instances of each template. Every truck gets one PM Schedule record for each template (154 per truck). The schedule tracks when it was last done and calculates the next due date.

7.3 Adding a New Task Template

Responsible Role: Admin or Super Admin

  1. Navigate to Sidebar → PM Templates.
  2. Click "Add Template".
  3. Complete the form:
    • Task Name: Clear, descriptive name (e.g., "Inspect and Adjust Air Brake Slack Adjuster").
    • Category: Select the appropriate category.
    • Interval Value: Numeric value of the interval (e.g., 5000).
    • Interval Unit: km, hrs, days, or "visual."
    • Description: Optional detailed notes about what this task entails.
  4. Click "Save".
  5. After saving: Sync existing trucks to create PM Schedules for the new template (see Chapter 5.4).

Chapter 8: Step-by-Step PM Procedures

This chapter contains the complete step-by-step procedures for all 23 PM categories (34 total procedures). Every category now has a dedicated detailed procedure covering inspection steps, diagnostics, and acceptance criteria. Each procedure follows a standardized format with safety precautions, required tools, detailed steps, acceptance criteria, and documentation requirements.

All procedures assume the following General Safety Rules apply at all times:

  • Wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): safety glasses, steel-toe boots, gloves, and high-visibility vest.
  • Ensure the truck is parked on level ground with the parking brake engaged.
  • Chock the wheels before working under or around the truck.
  • For tanker trucks: confirm the compartment is empty and gas-free before any hot work or entry.
  • Use lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures when working on electrical, hydraulic, or pneumatic systems.
  • Do not work alone on hazardous tasks — always have a spotter or second mechanic present.

    Chapter 9: PM Documentation & Record-Keeping

    9.1 Importance of Documentation

    Proper documentation of every PM task is essential for:

    • Legal compliance: Records of PM completion prove that the truck was maintained according to regulatory requirements.
    • Depot access: Many petroleum depots require proof of PM compliance before allowing access.
    • Trend analysis: PM history helps identify recurring issues and plan more effective maintenance strategies.
    • Resale value: Complete maintenance records increase the resale value of the truck.
    • Warranty compliance: Many manufacturer warranties require proof of scheduled maintenance.

    9.2 What Should Be Documented

    After each PM procedure, ensure the following is recorded in the Job Order line item or the PM Schedule note:

    • Date and time of completion.
    • Mileage and engine hours at the time of completion.
    • Parts used (with part numbers and quantities).
    • Actual labor hours spent on the task.
    • Observations: any unexpected findings, measurements taken, or follow-up actions needed.
    • Mechanic's name who performed the work.
    • Lead mechanic or supervisor verification (for critical systems: brakes, steering, suspension, chassis).

    9.3 When PM is Performed Outside the Job Order System

    If a PM task is performed outside the Job Order system (e.g., at a third-party shop, roadside service, or by the driver), the PM Schedule can be updated manually:

    1. Navigate to the truck detail page in the system.
    2. Find the PM Schedule section.
    3. Locate the task that was performed and click the schedule number (the underlined ID).
    4. Update last_completed_at, last_mileage_km, and last_engine_hours to reflect the service that was done.
    5. Click "Save". The system will automatically recalculate the next due date.

    Important: Manual updates should include a note in the schedule record explaining what was done and why it was not processed through a Job Order. This maintains an accurate audit trail.

    9.4 Using the ✅ Mark Complete Button

    For items on the PM Schedules list, the ✅ Mark Complete button provides a streamlined workflow. This creates a ServiceLogEntry directly without requiring a Job Order. Use this for:

    • Simple PM tasks that do not involve repair work.
    • Driver-performed inspections and checks.
    • Visual-only tasks such as walk-around inspections.

    When using ✅ Mark Complete, record: mileage and engine hours, labor hours spent, any parts used (name, quantity, unit cost), and notes or observations.
